About Me

Originally from "Wild and Wonderful" West Virginia (according to the license plates, anyway), I spent most of my youth travelling up and down the East Coast from Vermont to Florida before settling in New York City for college. And like so many, I never really left.

In the years since, I completed internships in numerous progressively-minded think tanks and advocacy organizations, and went on to earn an MA in international affairs from the New School in 2019, developing a passion and expertise in peacebuilding, migration, and humanitarianism. Since 2013, I've been working in various capacities at the Russell Sage Foundation, one of the oldest grantmaking institutions dedicated to the improvement of living conditions in the US through social science research. Professionally, I strive to marry my varied experience in the philanthropic sector with a passion in peacebuilding in post-conflict societies, migration policy, and the continued advancement of liberal, democratic norms in the US and abroad.

Outside of work, I'm interested in, well, learning. I'm driven by constantly filling my head with new stuff and evolving, relishing in that same feeling of enlightenment I had when I started trying to figure out why Bush v. Gore was a thing eons ago. Fun fact: I more than once read a history textbook for fun.

When I'm away from a computer screen, I also enjoy exploring the city and its nearabouts on my bike, hitting up a nice cocktail lounge every now and then, and planning on when I'm finally going to sit down and write that memoir people keep telling me I should get on with.

I currently live in Brooklyn with my partner, my cat, and an ever-growing family of low-light and drought-resistant houseplants.
